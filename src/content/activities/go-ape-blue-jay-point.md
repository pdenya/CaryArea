Most outdoor adventure is off the table during a heat wave — but Go Ape at Blue Jay Point is the exception, because the whole course lives up in the trees, under the forest canopy. Treetop ropes obstacles and ziplines, with actual shade doing the work a roof normally would.

This is the pick when the crew is itching for something that feels genuinely adventurous, not just another arcade. Clipping in, wobbling across rope bridges, and zipping between platforms is exactly the kind of shared adrenaline that turns a visit week into a story everyone retells at the holidays.

**Know before you go**

- Book ahead — this isn't a walk-up activity
- The ropes and ziplines run under forest canopy, so it's shaded — one of the few outdoor picks that survives this week's forecast
