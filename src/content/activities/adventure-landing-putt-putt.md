Sometimes the answer is just putt putt. Adventure Landing on Capital Boulevard has three separate 18-hole courses, so the nine-year-old, the eleven-year-olds, and the teens can all find a round that suits them — and if one course backs up, you hop to another. At around $10 a person, it's one of the cheaper ways to keep four kids and four adults entertained for an evening.

The courses are outdoors, so this is strictly a morning-or-evening play during the heat wave — save it for after dinner when the pavement stops radiating. If you'd rather stay closer to home, Frankie's Fun Park has the best mini golf near Cary and you can duck inside for arcade AC between holes.

**Know before you go**
- Three 18-hole courses, ~$10 per person
- 3311 Capital Blvd, Raleigh
- Outdoor course — go morning or evening only in this heat
- Alternative: Frankie's mini golf is the best near Cary
