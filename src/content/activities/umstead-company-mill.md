When Triangle folks are asked for a shaded hike, this is the default answer. Skip the full 5.8-mile loop — that's too long for younger kids — and instead do the out-and-back to Crabtree Creek and the old mill dam ruins, about 2.5 miles with plenty of rock-hopping to keep the kids busy. If legs are already tired, the Pott's Branch Loop is a gentle 1.3-mile alternative.

Get to the trailhead between 8 and 9:30am — the Reedy Creek lot fills by about 10 — or save it for after 5:30pm. Either way, the correct move afterward is Goodberry's frozen custard.

**Know before you go**

- Out-and-back to the mill ruins: ~2.5 mi; Pott's Branch Loop: 1.3 mi; full loop: 5.8 mi (skip with younger kids)
- Free; Reedy Creek lot fills by ~10am
- Rock-hopping at Crabtree Creek
- Do tick checks afterward
