Every city has a handful of places that have simply always been there, and Player's Retreat is one of Raleigh's — an old-Raleigh institution, the kind of spot locals mention when they want to show a visitor what the city was like before the boom. That makes it a natural pick with family in town: lunch with a side of local character.

The key detail from the locals: it's family-friendly at lunch. Time it right and you get the institution experience with the whole seven-person crew in tow, then you're back in the AC of the car before the afternoon sun does its worst.

**Know before you go**

- Go at lunch — that's when it's family-friendly
- It's a piece of old Raleigh, which is the whole point
