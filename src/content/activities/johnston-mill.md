This is the low-stakes, high-delight option: a roughly 2-mile shaded loop along the creek, past old mill ruins, and it's flat the whole way — no complaints from the younger kids, no climb to slow down grandparent-paced walkers. The real draw for the kids is critter-hunting in New Hope Creek; hand them a good stick and let the salamander search begin.

Like everything this week, time it for the cool bookends of the day: hit the trail between 8 and 9:30am, or come after 5:30pm when the shade does its best work. Because it's short and flat, it works great as a half-morning outing with the rest of the day left for the pool.

**Know before you go**

- ~2-mile shaded creek loop, flat
- Free
- Mill ruins along the way; critter-hunting in New Hope Creek
- Do tick checks afterward
