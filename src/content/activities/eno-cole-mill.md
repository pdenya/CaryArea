If the whole crew can only do one shady hike this week, this is the one. The trail follows the river the entire way under full canopy, with wading spots everywhere, and locals swear riverside shade feels ten degrees cooler. The payoff is Bobbitt's Hole — a real swimming hole with rocks to lay out on, and it's less crowded than the quarry. Kids from grade-schoolers up to teens all get something out of it.

Hit the trailhead between 8 and 9:30am, or come back after 5:30pm once the worst of the heat breaks. Bring water shoes and a towel.

**Know before you go**

- Free entry; park at the Cole Mill Access
- Full canopy the whole way, with wading all along the river
- Skip the Eno Quarry with kids — locals call it "deadly even"
- West Point on the Eno is closed July 2026–Jan 2027 for renovation, so don't detour there
- Do tick checks afterward
