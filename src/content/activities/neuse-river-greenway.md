Trade boots for bikes on this one. "Most of it is shaded," say the locals — a claim that picked up ten upvotes, which around here counts as peer review. The greenway follows the Neuse, and on a bike the crew generates its own breeze, which is about the best you can do in a heat wave short of jumping in water.

Ride it in the morning or the evening — same rule as the trailheads, out by 8–9:30am or after 5:30pm — and don't push through midday. The genius move for the kids: finish the ride and head to the Knightdale Station splash pad, so the hot-and-sweaty phase ends in shrieking, soaking-wet triumph.

**Know before you go**

- Free; start near Falls Dam
- Mostly shaded along the river
- Ride morning or evening, not midday
- Pair with the Knightdale Station splash pad afterward
