Duke Gardens is one of the Triangle's genuine treasures, and it's free — you only pay for parking. The koi pond is reliable kid magic, and the Asiatic Arboretum offers real shade, which is what makes this outing survivable in a week like yours. It's the kind of place where the adults get their beautiful-stroll fix while the kids hunt fish and turtles.

The whole plan hinges on one rule: arrive at 9am sharp, when the gates open, and be done by 11 before the sun takes over. Two hours is plenty to see the good stuff. It pairs naturally with Duke Chapel on a Saturday morning, when the chapel opens early enough to combine the two.

**Know before you go**
- Free admission; parking is $2/hr
- Open 9am–7pm — but arrive at 9am sharp, done by 11
- Head for the koi pond and the shaded Asiatic Arboretum
- Pairs with Duke Chapel on a Saturday morning
