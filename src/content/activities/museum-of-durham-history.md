Every vacation week needs a few of these in the back pocket: small, free, air-conditioned, and zero-commitment. The Museum of Durham History is exactly that — a compact downtown stop with a kids' play area, ideal for the awkward gap between lunch and whatever's next, or for cooling off mid-errand without buying seven tickets to anything.

Think of it as a 45-minute filler rather than a destination. If you're already in Durham for the Nasher, American Tobacco Campus, or a treats run to The Parlour, this slides right into the itinerary and costs you nothing but a little parking.

**Know before you go**

- Free admission, air-conditioned
- Small — plan on about 45 minutes
- Kids' play area keeps the younger two busy
- Best used as a filler stop on a Durham day
