Here's a double-header that costs exactly nothing: NC State's JC Raulston Arboretum and fishing at Lake Raleigh, both free and both on campus. The arboretum gives the adults a proper garden wander, and the lake gives the kids something to actually do — there's a particular kind of quiet triumph in an eleven-year-old landing a fish while the grown-ups admire plants.

It made the list from the free-and-quirky research pile, and it earns its spot as a zero-cost, low-planning outing you can drop into any morning or evening slot. The one rule to know: anyone 16 or older needs a fishing license to wet a line, so the teens and adults should sort that out ahead of time — the under-16s fish free and paperwork-free.

**Know before you go**
- Both free, both at NC State
- Fishing license required for ages 16 and up
- Morning or evening only in this heat
