A playground parked right beside RDU's runway, where jets thunder in and out close enough to feel it. It's free, and it's practically engineered for grade-schoolers and tweens: they climb, they play, and every few minutes the whole park stops to watch a plane roar past.

This is a zero-planning outing — no tickets, no reservations, no agenda. Since it's all outdoors, aim for a morning or an evening when the heat backs off; plane-spotting at golden hour is its own kind of magic. Bring water bottles and let them run until the novelty wears off (it won't).

**Know before you go**

- Completely free
- Playground plus runway plane-spotting — perfect for younger kids
- Outdoor — go morning or evening, not peak afternoon, during this heat wave
