Consider this the budget answer to the water park question. Wake Forest's Holding Park Aquatic Center has slides — the thing kids actually care about — at a non-resident price of just $6–7 a head. For a big group, that's a full afternoon of splashing for less than one person's ticket at the big parks.

It made the list as the designated "cheap slide day": the outing you reach for when the crew wants more excitement than the neighborhood pool but the week's budget has already taken hits from pontoons and inflatable quarry parks. The younger kids get their slide fix, the teens get water deep enough to be worth their time, and the adults get to cool off without wincing at the receipt. Simple math, happy kids.

**Know before you go**

- Non-resident admission $6–7
- Slides on site — the draw over a plain pool
- The cheap-slide-day pick on the water list
