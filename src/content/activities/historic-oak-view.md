When Raleigh locals on Reddit were asked for hidden gems, this was the one that kept coming up. Historic Oak View is a preserved farmstead tucked into east Raleigh — goats to greet, fruit trees to wander past, and a small cotton museum that sneaks a little history into the visit. And it's completely free.

It's the kind of unhurried, no-agenda stop that works beautifully for a mixed-age crew: the younger kids gravitate to the animals, and the adults get a genuinely charming slice of old North Carolina. Since the farmstead is outdoors, treat it like the rest of the outdoor list this week — morning or evening, not the blazing middle of the day.

**Know before you go**

- Free admission
- Goats, fruit trees, farmstead buildings, and a cotton museum
- Outdoor — go in a morning or evening window during the heat wave
