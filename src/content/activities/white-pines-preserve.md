When the forecast is genuinely brutal, drive to the coolest microclimate in the area. A stand of relict white pines keeps this preserve noticeably cooler than everywhere else, and the roughly 4-mile loop takes you down to where the Deep and Rocky rivers meet, with wading at the old cable-bridge spot. Fair warning for the crew: these are real hills, so it's better suited to the 11-and-ups and teens than anyone who's already dragging.

It's about 45 minutes away, so leave early enough to hit the trail in the 8–9:30am window (or go after 5:30pm). The reward for the drive: S&T's Soda Shoppe in Pittsboro on the way home.

**Know before you go**

- ~4-mile loop to the Deep/Rocky river confluence, with real hills
- Free; about 45 minutes from Cary
- Wading at the old cable-bridge spot
- Pair with S&T's Soda Shoppe in Pittsboro
- Do tick checks afterward
