This is the centerpiece of the week, and the itinerary practically writes itself. Thursday July 9 is Boardwalk Blast night at Carolina Beach: live music on the boardwalk 6:30–9:30pm and free fireworks over the ocean at 9pm. That built-in evening anchor is exactly why Carolina Beach won. Wrightsville has prettier water but brutal parking ($25–30/day, lots full by 9–10am), no kid anchor, and a college-crowd skew. Atlantic Beach has the family vibe and a sleeper hit in Fort Macon, but it's farther (~2h30) with no boardwalk.

**The plan:** Leave Cary Thursday around 9:30–10am, after rush hour, and request early check-in. Beach and boardwalk all afternoon, rides and arcade into the evening, then music and fireworks. Friday morning: Britt's Donuts on the boardwalk — legendary, and cash only — then the NC Aquarium at Fort Fisher, 15 minutes south (~$15 adult / $11 kid, open 9–5, book online). Leave Kure Beach by about 2pm to beat the Friday rush back into Raleigh, home by 5 with the Koka Booth concert still on the table that night.

**Lodging (two options, book today):**
- Courtyard by Marriott Carolina Beach Oceanfront — ~$300–450/room; two rooms ≈ $650–900, walkable to the boardwalk
- Hampton Inn & Suites Carolina Beach Oceanfront — similar pricing; suites sleep 5–6, so one suite plus one room could work

**Know before you go**

- Book the hotel immediately — Thursday night helps availability
- Bring cash for Britt's Donuts
- Book aquarium tickets online
- Parking is paid (~$5/hr) but more plentiful than Wrightsville
